01 — Brand Foundation

Who Vastoma Is

Vastoma is an enterprise technology company building intelligent software systems that scale. Every design choice reflects three core promises: structured intelligence, quiet confidence, and active execution.

Mission

To help global enterprises build smarter software faster — through scalable architectures, AI-assisted engineering, and disciplined execution.

Vision

A world where every business operates on intelligent, future-proof systems that grow without breaking.

Tagline

Build smart. Scale further.

Used in all formal communication, never modified.

Five Brand Values

① Clarity

Every screen, sentence, and interaction must be obvious without explanation.

② Precision

Details matter. Engineering quality shows in pixels and words alike.

③ Intelligence

Smart by default — products think ahead, designs anticipate the user.

④ Restraint

Less, but better. We earn attention; we don’t shout for it.

03 — Clear Space & Minimum Size

Breathing Room

The logo must always be surrounded by a minimum protected area equal to the height of the bracket icon (X = 44px standard). Never crop, crowd, or place on busy imagery without an overlay.

Clear Space Rule · 1X Padding

Reserve 1X on all sides where X = icon height (44px).

04 — Logo Misuse

How Not to Use the Logo

Consistency builds trust. These rules are non-negotiable in any external communication.

✓ Do

Use approved logo files only. Maintain clear space. Place on solid Navy, White, or Gold backgrounds. Keep proportions locked.

✗ Don’t

Stretch, rotate, recolor, add drop shadows, glow effects, gradients, outlines, or place on low-contrast / busy photographs.

✓ Do

Use the dark-mode variant on backgrounds darker than #1F2937. Use icon-only on tight spaces (favicon, app, social avatar).

✗ Don’t

Recreate the wordmark in another typeface. Change the icon color combination. Animate the cursor blink in static contexts.

06 — Light Mode & Dark Mode

Mode-Aware Tokens

Both modes share the same brand core (Navy, Gold, Red) but swap surface and text tokens. Always reference tokens — never hardcode hex values in components.

Mode Switching Rule
In dark mode, the primary action color flips from Navy to Gold — because Navy disappears against dark surfaces. The Gold accent stays consistent in both modes as the brand’s visual signature.

19 — Iconography

Icon Style

Use Lucide Icons exclusively. Stroke 1.75px, rounded line-caps, 24×24 viewbox. Never mix icon sets.

Icon Rules
Always pair icons with labels in primary navigation. Use currentColor for stroke so icons inherit text color. Never decorate icons with shadows or gradients.

20 — Photography & Imagery

Visual Voice

Imagery communicates ambition, precision, and human impact. Every photo must feel editorial and purposeful.

✓ Do
  • High-contrast, dramatic lighting — editorial feel
  • Real teams in real working environments
  • Tech + human connection in same frame
  • Architectural photography with clean lines
  • Desaturated or duotone overlays (Navy/Gold)
  • Wide-angle establishing shots for sections
  • Close-ups that show precision and craft
  • Consistent warm-to-dark tone grading
✗ Don’t
  • Generic stock photos of handshakes or clipart
  • Oversaturated, HDR-processed images
  • Low-resolution images under 72 dpi for screen
  • Busy, cluttered scenes with no focal point
  • Mismatched tones across a single page
  • Comic-style illustrations not from brand set
  • White backgrounds on isolated objects (use color block)
  • Photos with visible watermarks or third-party branding

21 — Motion Design System

Animation Language

Motion communicates hierarchy and state — not decoration. All animations respect prefers-reduced-motion.

Duration Tokens

--dur-fast100msMicro
--dur-base200msDefault
--dur-slow350msReveals
--dur-page600msPage load

Easing Tokens

--ease-outcubic-bezier(.22,1,.36,1)
--ease-incubic-bezier(.5,0,1,.5)
--ease-bouncecubic-bezier(.68,-.6,.32,1.6)
--ease-linearlinear

Principles

  • Enter fast, exit faster
  • Never animate layout shifts
  • Favor opacity + transform only
  • Max 3 animated elements per screen
  • Respect reduced-motion always
  • Cursor blink: 1.0s step-end loop

23 — Brand Voice & Tone

How We Speak

Vastoma sounds like a trusted senior engineer who also understands business. Confident, precise, and direct. Never jargon-heavy or overly corporate.

Confident

We make definitive statements. We don’t hedge with “maybe” or “might”.

Do: “We cut your deployment time by half.”
Don’t: “We might help improve your processes somewhat.”
Precise

We use specific numbers, real outcomes, and concrete examples. Avoid vague claims.

Do: “Trusted by 2,800+ engineering teams globally.”
Don’t: “Loved by countless companies worldwide.”
Human

We talk to people, not at them. Conversational without being casual.

Do: “Your team deserves tools that keep up.”
Don’t: “Leverage synergistic outcomes through our platform.”
Ambitious

We think big but earn it with substance. Ambition backed by proof.

Do: “Built for teams that refuse to ship slow.”
Don’t: “We’re the world’s best platform.” (no proof)
Writing Mechanics
• Sentence case everywhere except proper nouns and CTAs.
• Em dashes (—) with no spaces (not hyphens) for parenthetical clauses.
• Oxford comma always.
• No exclamation marks in marketing copy. Save for genuine moments only.
• Ampersand (&) only in UI tight spaces; spell “and” in body copy.
• Percentage sign (%) is fine. Dollar sign ($) OK.
