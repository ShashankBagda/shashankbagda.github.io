01 — Brand Foundation

Who Shomiel Is

Shomiel is an enterprise technology company building intelligent software systems that scale. Every design choice in this guide reflects our three core promises: structured intelligence, quiet confidence, and active execution.

Mission

To help global enterprises build smarter software faster — through scalable architectures, AI-assisted engineering, and disciplined execution.

Vision

A world where every business — regardless of size — operates on intelligent, future-proof systems that grow without breaking.

Tagline

Build smart. Scale further.

Used in all formal communication, never modified.

Five Brand Values

① Clarity

Every screen, sentence, and interaction must be obvious without explanation.

② Precision

Details matter. Engineering quality shows in pixels and words alike.

③ Intelligence

Smart by default — products think ahead, designs anticipate the user.

④ Restraint

Less, but better. We earn attention; we don't shout for it.

Icon Concept · What It Means
The icon is a code bracket [ ] — the universal symbol of software — containing three horizontal lines representing data streams and system layers (frontend, logic, infrastructure). 03 — Clear Space & Minimum Size

Breathing Room

The logo must always be surrounded by a minimum protected area equal to the height of the bracket icon (X). Never crop, crowd, or place the logo on busy imagery without an overlay.

Clear Space Rule · 1X Padding

Reserve 1X on all sides where X = icon height (44px in standard).

04 — Logo Misuse · The Don'ts

How Not to Use the Logo

Consistency builds trust. These rules are non-negotiable in any external communication.

✓ Do

Use approved logo files only. Maintain clear space. Place on solid Navy, White, or Gold backgrounds. Keep proportions locked.

✗ Don't

Stretch, rotate, recolor, add drop shadows, glow effects, gradients, outlines, or place on low-contrast / busy photographs.

✓ Do

Use the dark-mode variant on backgrounds darker than #1F2937. Use icon-only on tight spaces (favicon, app, social avatar).

✗ Don't

Recreate the wordmark in another typeface. Change the icon color combination. Animate the cursor blink in static contexts.

Mode Switching Rule

19 — Iconography

Icon Style

Use Lucide Icons exclusively. Stroke 1.75px, rounded line-caps, 24×24 viewbox. Never mix icon sets.

Icon Rules
Always pair icons with labels in primary navigation. Use currentColor for stroke so icons inherit text color. Never decorate icons with shadows or gradients.
20 — Imagery & Photography

Visual Tone

Imagery is architectural, abstract, and engineered — never stock-photo cliché.

✓ Yes — Use
  • ● Clean architectural photography (concrete, glass, steel)
  • ● Macro shots of circuit boards, fiber optics, code editors
  • ● Abstract data visualizations on dark backgrounds
  • ● Real engineers / customers in their natural environments
  • ● Duotone treatments using Navy + Gold
✗ No — Avoid
  • ● Stock photos of people pointing at screens
  • ● Cliché handshakes, lightbulbs, jigsaw puzzles
  • ● Rainbow gradients, neon, cyberpunk aesthetics
  • ● Over-saturated or HDR imagery
  • ● 3D-rendered AI brains or robotic faces
21 — Motion Principles

Movement With Purpose

Motion exists to clarify, never to decorate. Three durations, two easings — that's it.

Durations

--dur-fast 150ms — micro-interactions (hover, focus)
--dur-base 250ms — defaults (open, fade)
--dur-slow 400ms — large transitions (page, modal)

Easings

--ease-out — entrances, expansions
--ease-in-out — state changes, slides

Never use linear easing for UI.

Principles

① Always purposeful
② Never longer than 400ms in UI
③ Respect prefers-reduced-motion
④ Animate transform + opacity only (never width/height)

23 — Voice & Tone

How We Sound

Shomiel sounds like a senior engineer who respects your time. Clear, confident, never corporate.

① Clear, not clever

Plain English beats jargon every time.

"We deploy in 14 days."
"We leverage agile synergies."
② Confident, not arrogant

Make the claim, then prove it.

"Built for scale. Tested at 10M req/s."
"World's best platform."
③ Specific, not vague

Numbers, names, and verbs.

"Cut deployment time by 73%."
"Significantly faster deployments."
④ Human, not corporate

Write like you're talking to one engineer.

"Here's how it works."
"Please find enclosed our solution overview."
Writing Mechanics
• Sentence case for headings (not Title Case).
• Oxford comma always.
• Numerals for numbers ≥ 10. Spell out one through nine.
• Active voice over passive.
• Avoid: "robust", "synergy", "leverage", "best-in-class", "seamless", "next-gen".
